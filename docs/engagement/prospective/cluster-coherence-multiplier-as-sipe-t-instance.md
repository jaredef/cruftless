## 1. Statement

The 2026-05-25 cruftless session produced a substrate move whose tests-per-locale-instance ratio was roughly 19x the same session's prior seven parser-arc closures. Inspection of the difference identified five named conditions whose conjunction induces the multiplier; absence of any one contracts it proportionally. The recognition the keeper named is that this is not a local empirical accident but the substrate-engineering-engagement-tier instance of a structural pattern the corpus has already articulated at three other tiers.

The pattern: properties of a substrate move are not intrinsic to the move itself; they are induced by the composition of named constraints the move inhabits. Adding a constraint to the move's inhabited stack induces a property; removing one contracts the property set. The pattern is the corpus's prior SIPE-T (Doc 541) restated at the substrate-engineering tier, with Fielding-style constraint accumulation (Doc 419's PRESTO instance) as the structural template applied to a different domain.

This document specifies the cluster-coherence multiplier as a SIPE-T instance, names its five conditions explicitly, places it in the corpus's existing lineage rather than claiming novel patterning, and supplies a falsifier-tier prediction operating on the not-yet-worked locales in the top-10 spawn batch (`pilots/temporal-availability/` plus eight sibling locales spawned 2026-05-25).

## 3. The five conditions

The session's empirical observation, distilled. BBND-EXT 1+2 closed 95 test262 fixtures in two rungs (~140 LOC, with the second rung an R15 chapter-close-inspect surfaced sub-shape). The same session's earlier seven parser-arc closures averaged ~5 tests per locale. The 19x amortization difference is attributable to the conjunction of five named conditions on the BBND locale's inhabited stack.

### Condition 1 — Spec-rule-to-directory bijection

The test262 sub-directory `language/block-scope/syntax/redeclaration/` corresponds to exactly one spec production (§13.2.1 Block static-semantics early errors). The TC39 conformance team organized the directory to enumerate that single rule's full surface. When a test262 sub-directory bijects to a single spec rule, the directory's fixture count is the rule's full enumerated surface. Implementing the rule closes the directory.

By contrast, today's earlier closures targeted production-level edges (a single grammar discrimination, a single lookahead constraint). Production-level edges do not enjoy the bijection property; their tests are scattered across the sub-directory.

### Condition 2 — Cross-product test generation

The fixtures are generated procedurally (test262 flag `[generated]`). The author enumerated 8 declaration-kinds × several positional shapes ≈ 95 fixtures from one rule. Generated cross-products multiply spec-rule yield by the product of the enumerated axes. Non-generated tests yield a small constant per spec-rule (~1-5).

### Condition 3 — One-rule-one-site implementation

§13.2.1 fires at one syntactic site: the Block production's static-semantics phase. The substrate move is one check inserted at one parse function. Multi-site rules cost more LOC per test closed; coordination across multiple sites also introduces R6 surface-completeness audit overhead (Doc 743 ruleset).

### Condition 4 — Apparatus tier above the production

§13.2.1 is a static-semantics rule, not a grammar production. Static-semantics rules have cross-cutting reach across multiple syntactic surfaces; the same rule fires for let/let, const/let, class/var, function/var in non-strict, and so on. Implementing the rule once captures all these inputs.

Production-tier rules are narrow by construction. The yield-per-LOC is dominated by the coordinate's reach, not by the edit's size. The full-suite Pin-Art apparatus's reading: production-tier coordinates are high-resolution but narrow; static-semantics-tier coordinates are lower-resolution but cross-cutting.

### Condition 5 — Matrix-driven coordinate targeting

The full-suite Pin-Art matrix (per the new apparatus articulation `apparatus/docs/ecma-conformance-parity-as-exhaustive-language-behavior-dag.md`) names coordinates. The PEER row-coherence inspection (debugging-heuristics §V) identified the shared mechanism across multiple sampled fails (3/3 inspected, BoundNames duplication) before any code was edited.

Coordinate-shaped work captures full cluster surfaces. Probe-shaped work captures probe-adjacent surfaces only. The matrix is the apparatus that surfaces coordinate-shaped opportunities; the heuristics §V protocol is the discipline that converts a candidate row to a substrate move.

### Composition

Each of the five conditions is individually a 2-5x multiplier on tests-per-locale. They compose multiplicatively. When all five hold: ~95 tests per locale. When three or four hold (today's larger parser closures like FHAPV at 16 tests): 10-30 tests per locale. When one or two hold (narrow production-edge closures like FAOF at 1 test): 1-3 tests per locale.

The composition is the substrate-engineering instance of Doc 541's threshold-conditional pattern. The order parameter is the conjunction-count of named conditions; the induced property is the tests-per-locale yield.

## 6. The corollary for engagement discipline

The recognition has an operational corollary: **constraint visibility is yield discipline**. A locale's seed.md should name the constraints its inhabited stack imposes BEFORE the apparatus tax (seed + trajectory + manifest + agent-feedback) is paid; absent that naming, the induced-property set cannot be predicted before the work lands, and locale-spawn decisions degrade to count-driven heuristics.

The full-suite Pin-Art apparatus's debugging-heuristics doc (§III) already warns against count-driven decisions in different language: "compare rows only inside a partition." The partition is the constraint partition; rows comparable inside a partition share inhabited-stack conditions. This document supplies the corpus articulation for why the partition matters.

For each prospective locale, enumerate at seed time:

- Which named conditions the locale's inhabited stack will satisfy.
- Which conditions it will not satisfy and why.
- The predicted yield range from the satisfied conditions' product.

If three or more named conditions from a shared catalog hold, the locale will yield multiplicatively and a fresh locale-spawn is warranted. If fewer, land as a nested rung inside an existing locale; the apparatus tax does not amortize against single-rung yields.

The catalog for cluster-shaped locales is the five conditions above. The catalog for performance-shaped locales is standing rule 11's five coverage axes (component A/B, op-set coverage, value-domain coverage, locals-marshaling coverage, emission-shape coverage). The catalog for capability-shaped locales is Doc 736's authority-composition predicates (no-ambient-authority, sealed-handles, closed-import-graph, load-time-integrity). Each catalog is a five-condition family in its respective substrate tier.
